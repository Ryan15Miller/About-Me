Citizen Legal Duties

Civic Responsibilities

Serving Jury Duty

Your right of trial by jury is kept alive by you being a juror. Because if no one ever showed up to the court for a hearing there would obviously be no way your right of trial by jury. Trial by jury helps to protect people from wrongly being accused of something that they didn't do.

Obeying the Law

A society could not function if anyone did anything they wanted. There needs to be some type of order to keep people safe and free. Without order there would be no way the government could still stand thus it could not protect any of your rights.

Attending School

Mandatory schooling creates more informed and educated voters to pass better laws for everyone. It also promotes economic growth because of a better distribution of wealth. A reason it was enacted was there we're so many children working in dangerous conditions while they worked in factories at a very young age.

Paying Taxes

Paying taxes is required because it pays for many things to keep you safe and free on a daily basis. Just some of the things it pays for is a military, a police force, a fire department, and many other government agencies and branches. Something else taxes pay for than an individual cannot are roads that everyone can use without outrageous tolls.

Voting

Voting is a right and a privilege in the United States it gives the citizens a voice in the government. It gets laws passed that at least some people like and representatives that should have the same values as the majority of the people the represent.

Being Informed

Being an informed citizen keeps the government from being overpowered. By the people knowing what they are voting for or what kind of person they are voting for it betters the nation because there are less corrupt laws. One who knows what is going on around them will make better decisions.

"Our citizens - naturalized or native-born - must also seek to refresh and improve their knowledge of how our government operates under the Constitution and how they can participate in it. Only in this way can they assume the full responsibilities of citizenship and make our government more truly of, by, and for the people." - Lyndon B. Johnson

Respecting and Protecting Others Rights

With so much diversity in Democracy it is important that everyone is respected so people can get along to do other civic responsibilities. Also other people respect your rights so why not do the same to others. If people are deprived of their rights they will probably try to get them back however they can which may go against other duties or responsibilities.

Helping the Community

Bettering the community is important to keeping the society functioning. Because without people trying to better the community it would likely fall apart. Because without local government employees and other organizations to help communities it would probably be more dangerous and not a good place to live.

Volunteering

You should volunteer because so many of the civic responsibilities can be covered with just doing a few small things. If you have done a lot of volunteering it shows that you're committed to the community and a willing to work for the betterment of others.
